After an injury, surrounding tissue and scar can sometimes irritate or entrap small superficial nerves, contributing to localised pain, burning or altered sensation. Perineural injection therapy is one approach used to address this type of nerve-related pain.

The treatment involves a series of small injections of a dilute glucose solution placed around the affected superficial nerves. The aim is to reduce nerve irritation and the associated pain. Treatment is usually given over several sessions, and the response varies between individuals. The evidence base for perineural injection therapy is still developing, and whether it is appropriate for you is discussed at consultation alongside the alternatives.
