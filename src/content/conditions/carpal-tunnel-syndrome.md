Carpal tunnel syndrome is a common nerve entrapment condition at the wrist. Symptoms often occur at night or during activities that involve gripping, vibration or sustained wrist position.

Assessment focuses on confirming whether the median nerve is the likely source and checking for weakness, muscle wasting or other neurological findings. Other causes of hand symptoms, including cervical radiculopathy, need to be considered when the pattern is unclear.

Non-surgical care may include night splinting, reducing aggravating loads, ergonomic changes and selected ultrasound-guided procedures. Persistent or progressive neurological deficit needs timely review because prolonged nerve compression can become harder to reverse.
