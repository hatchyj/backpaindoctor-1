Open with the question the patient is actually asking, and give them the short answer in the first paragraph. People (and search engines) reward a clear, direct lead.

## A useful subheading

Explain the reasoning. Keep it outcome-honest — describe what a treatment *aims* to do and what the evidence does and doesn't support. Where evidence is mixed, say so plainly.

## Another subheading

Link internally where relevant — for example to the [relevant condition page](/conditions) or a [treatment page](/non-surgical-treatments). This helps readers and supports SEO.

## When to seek care

Close with practical guidance: what the reader can do, and when they should see a GP or seek urgent review.
