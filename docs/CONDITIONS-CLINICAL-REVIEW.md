# Conditions clinical review

This review applies to the `/conditions` section for The Back Pain Doctor site.

## Clinical positioning

The condition pages should be diagnosis-first MSK education pages, not treatment landing pages. The reader should understand:

- what the condition usually represents clinically
- common symptom patterns
- when imaging may help and when it may overlead decision-making
- why load management and rehabilitation usually sit at the centre of care
- when procedures may be considered as adjuncts rather than default treatments
- when symptoms need urgent or specialist review

## Evidence-based writing rules

1. Avoid implying that a scan finding automatically explains pain.
2. Avoid implying that an injection, shockwave, PRP, EMTT or prolotherapy is first-line for every condition.
3. Use language such as "may be considered", "selected cases", "depending on assessment" and "as part of a broader plan" where procedural treatments are discussed.
4. Put education, reassurance, load modification, exercise and rehabilitation before procedural treatments for most conditions.
5. For osteoarthritis pages, avoid cure language. Emphasise exercise, weight/load management, symptom control and function.
6. For tendinopathy pages, emphasise progressive loading and capacity-building rather than rest alone or passive treatment.
7. For nerve presentations, include escalation language for progressive weakness, bladder/bowel symptoms, gait disturbance or severe/progressive neurological deficit.
8. For acute injury pages, distinguish normal pain from red flags such as major trauma, inability to weight bear, marked swelling/bruising, systemic illness or concern for DVT.
9. Avoid using treatment links as implied recommendations. Treatment links are educational options only.
10. Keep language Australian, practical and clinically conservative.

## Section-level changes completed

- Conditions index copy was revised to emphasise diagnosis-first, evidence-based MSK care.
- Single condition page template was revised so all treatment link sections state that education, load management and rehabilitation are usually first-line.
- Treatment link language now clarifies that linked procedures are educational options, not automatic recommendations.
- Conditions index sorting was made deterministic by sorting by `order` and then title.
